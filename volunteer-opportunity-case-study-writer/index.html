Case Study Writer – Ref: CSW-001

Overview

Terra has over 250 participants who have completed the Rotational Mineral Priming (RMP) program over the last three years. The Case Study Writer accesses partial participant files, follows up with individuals to document their experiences, and maintains accurate, anonymized records. This role requires discretion, consistency, and a genuine interest in participant outcomes.

Responsibilities

The Case Study Writer receives partial, anonymized files from Terra. The writer contacts past participants via email, phone, or WhatsApp to inquire about their experience, whether they still practice the sequence, how often, and what outcomes they have observed. All responses are recorded in a structured format, such as a spreadsheet or database. The writer then drafts clear, factual, anonymized case summaries at a rate of three to five per month. These summaries are proofread for errors and consistency before submission. The writer also updates the participant database and content management system with new information, changes, and follow-up dates.

Database and CMS Environment

The Case Study Writer will work with two primary tools.

Airtable. Terra uses Airtable as its participant database. Airtable is a cloud-based, spreadsheet-database hybrid that allows for structured data entry, filtering, and collaboration. The writer will have access to a view containing partial, anonymized participant records. Training on the specific view and fields will be provided. The writer will update participant records with follow-up notes, outcomes, and next follow-up dates directly within Airtable.

Website CMS. The Terra website is built on an open-source static content management system. The system offers a visual editing interface similar to traditional blogging platforms but generates static HTML files that can be deployed to any hosting provider. The writer will use this CMS to publish completed and approved case studies to the Terra website. This involves updating sections of posts, formatting public content using the built-in editor, adding featured images if available, and syncing changes to the live site via one-click deployment.

Workload and Commitment

The Case Study Writer completes three to five participant follow-ups per month, requiring approximately two to three hours of work per week. A monthly stipend of KSh 7,000 is provided. The role is ongoing, with a minimum three-month commitment preferred. The position is fully remote and open to applicants in Kenya.

What the Role Does Not Include

The Case Study Writer is not required to interpret medical data. The role is to document what participants report, not to analyze or diagnose. The writer does not recruit new participants; this role focuses solely on existing participants. Social media management is a separate role. The writer does not handle sensitive medical records; Terra provides only partial, anonymized files. The role is designed to require no more than three hours of work per week, making it manageable alongside other commitments.

Compensation and Benefits

The Case Study Writer receives a monthly stipend of KSh 7,000, paid via M-Pesa or bank transfer. The writer gains experience building a portfolio of case studies and participant documentation. A reference letter from the Terra founder or research coordinator is provided after three months. After six months of consistent work, the writer may become eligible for additional responsibilities. The role also develops skills in interviewing, writing, proofreading, and database management.

Self-Assessment Questions

Prospective candidates should ask themselves whether they can write clearly and proofread their own work. They should be comfortable calling or messaging people they do not know. They must be able to commit two to three hours every week consistently. They must understand the difference between documenting participant reports and interpreting those reports medically. They must handle participant information with discretion. If the answer to all these questions is yes, they may be the right person for the role.

How to Apply

Send an email to info@terrain.co.ke with the subject line: Ref: CSW-001 – [Your Name]

Include the following:

  • Cover letter (brief, 1-2 paragraphs) explaining why you are interested in Terra and what makes you a good fit for this role
  • Curriculum Vitae (CV) highlighting your education, work experience, and any relevant writing or interviewing experience
  • Writing sample (500-800 words) – a blog post, article, academic essay, or any non-fiction piece that demonstrates your ability to write clearly and factually
  • Your weekly availability (estimated hours per week)

Response time: 5-7 business days. Only shortlisted candidates will be contacted.

Terra is an educational framework. It is not a medical treatment, diagnosis, or cure. Case study writers document participant-reported outcomes. They do not evaluate or interpret health results.
